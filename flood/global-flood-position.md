# Global Flood Position

## Position Summary

The flood in Genesis 6-9 was global in geographic scope. It covered the earth, destroyed all land-dwelling humans and animals outside the ark, and stands as a universal judgment that anticipates final judgment.

This position should be presented first as a biblical and theological reading, not merely as a scientific or geological claim.

## Strongest Steel-Man

Thoughtful global-flood advocates are not merely trying to win a science argument. Their central concern is biblical coherence: Genesis' language, the purpose of the ark, the rainbow covenant, and the New Testament's use of Noah all seem to them to pull in the same direction.

## Argument 1: Repeated Universal Language

Primary biblical texts:

- [Genesis 6:17](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Genesis+6%3A17&version=NRSVUE)
- [Genesis 7:19-23](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Genesis+7%3A19-23&version=NRSVUE)
- [Genesis 9:8-17](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Genesis+9%3A8-17&version=NRSVUE)

Advocates emphasize phrases such as all flesh, under heaven, all the high mountains, and every living thing. The claim is not based on one word in isolation but on accumulation.

Advocate sources:

- [John C. Whitcomb, "The Church and the Global Genesis Flood," Answers in Genesis](https://answersingenesis.org/the-flood/global/the-church-and-the-global-genesis-flood/)
- [Terry Mortenson, "Noah's Flood: a Unique, Historical, Yearlong, Global Catastrophe," Answers in Genesis](https://answersingenesis.org/the-flood/global/biblical-necessity-global-flood/)
- [Jonathan Sarfati, "Global Flood of Genesis," Creation Ministries International](https://creation.com/en/articles/global-flood-genesis)

Steel-man phrasing:

> "Global advocates argue that the repeated universal language is not incidental; Genesis seems to be slowing the reader down so the scope of judgment cannot be missed."

## Argument 2: The Ark Makes Best Sense As Global Rescue

Advocate sources:

- [Terry Mortenson, Answers in Genesis](https://answersingenesis.org/the-flood/global/biblical-necessity-global-flood/)
- [John Whitcomb, Answers in Genesis](https://answersingenesis.org/the-flood/global/the-church-and-the-global-genesis-flood/)

Claim:

- If the flood were only local, God could have commanded Noah, his family, and local animals to migrate.
- The ark's scale and preservation purpose make better sense if the catastrophe threatened all land-dwelling life relevant to the narrative.

Regional/local advocates have answers to this, especially around Noah's prophetic witness; see [regional-flood-position.md](regional-flood-position.md). But in the global reading, the ark is a major internal clue.

## Argument 3: Duration And Chronology

Primary biblical texts:

- [Genesis 7:11-12](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Genesis+7%3A11-12&version=NRSVUE)
- [Genesis 7:24-8:14](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Genesis+7%3A24-8%3A14&version=NRSVUE)

Claim:

- The flood narrative gives a long chronology, not merely a flash flood.
- Global advocates argue that the water's duration and recession pattern sound like a world-altering event.

Advocate source:

- [John Whitcomb, Answers in Genesis](https://answersingenesis.org/the-flood/global/the-church-and-the-global-genesis-flood/)

## Argument 4: The Rainbow Covenant

Primary biblical text:

- [Genesis 9:8-17](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Genesis+9%3A8-17&version=NRSVUE)

Advocate sources:

- [Henry M. Morris, "Why Christians Should Believe in a Global Flood," Institute for Creation Research](https://www.icr.org/content/why-christians-should-believe-global-flood)
- [John Whitcomb, Answers in Genesis](https://answersingenesis.org/the-flood/global/the-church-and-the-global-genesis-flood/)
- [Ligonier, "Signs of the Covenant"](https://learn.ligonier.org/devotionals/signs-covenant)

Claim:

- God promises never again to destroy all flesh with such a flood.
- If Noah's flood were merely a local flood, later local floods raise a theological question: what exactly did God promise never to repeat?

Steel-man phrasing:

> "For global advocates, the covenant sign is not a side issue. It raises the question: what kind of flood does the rainbow promise rule out?"

## Argument 5: New Testament Use

Primary biblical texts:

- [Matthew 24:37-39](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Matthew+24%3A37-39&version=NRSVUE)
- [Luke 17:26-27](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Luke+17%3A26-27&version=NRSVUE)
- [Hebrews 11:7](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Hebrews+11%3A7&version=NRSVUE)
- [1 Peter 3:18-22](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=1+Peter+3%3A18-22&version=NRSVUE)
- [2 Peter 2:5](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=2+Peter+2%3A5&version=NRSVUE)
- [2 Peter 3:5-7](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=2+Peter+3%3A5-7&version=NRSVUE)

Advocate sources:

- [Lita Sanders, "The global Flood - according to the New Testament," Creation Ministries International](https://creation.com/en/articles/nt-global-flood)
- [Andrew Snelling and Ken Ham, "Was the Flood of Noah Global or Local in Extent?" Answers in Genesis](https://answersingenesis.org/the-flood/global/was-the-flood-of-noah-global-or-local-in-extent/)
- [Answers in Genesis, "Noah's Flood: a Historical, Global Catastrophe"](https://answersingenesis.org/noahs-flood/)

Claim:

- Jesus and Peter use the flood as a paradigm of sudden judgment.
- 2 Peter 3 connects creation, flood judgment, and final judgment.
- Global advocates argue that the typological force is strongest when the flood was itself universal in scope.

Gracious note:

- Regional/local advocates can affirm the New Testament's theological use while disagreeing that typology requires modern planetary scope.

## Argument 7: Geological / Scientific Claims As Secondary Support

Advocate sources:

- [Henry Morris, ICR](https://www.icr.org/content/why-christians-should-believe-global-flood)
- [Tim Clarey, "The Genesis Flood: Upheaval in Uniformitarian Geology," ICR](https://www.icr.org/content/genesis-flood-upheaval-uniformitarian-geology)
- [Andrew Snelling, "The Flood: The Big Picture," Answers in Genesis](https://answersingenesis.org/media/video/bible/the-flood-big-picture/)

Common claims:

- Sedimentary strata and fossils are interpreted as evidence for rapid catastrophic burial.
- Conventional historical geology is challenged by a flood-geology framework.
- Some advocates argue for catastrophic plate tectonics or other large-scale mechanisms.

Facilitation caution:

- Keep geology secondary unless the group wants that debate. The study's purpose is unity, interpretation, and gracious disagreement, not a full technical science session.

## Gracious Discussion Notes

This view often sees itself as guarding:

- The plain sense of the repeated language.
- The unity of Genesis, covenant, and New Testament typology.
- The seriousness of divine judgment.
- The trustworthiness of Scripture against outside pressures.

Best discussion question:

> "What concern is the global-flood reading trying to protect, and where do you see that concern in the text?"
